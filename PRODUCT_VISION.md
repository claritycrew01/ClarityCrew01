# ClarityCrew — Product Vision

## What it is

ClarityCrew is a learning website designed around one core idea: **adapt the learning experience to how each person actually learns, not the other way around.** It is an academic study companion, not a disorder education app. It helps users build knowledge, stay engaged, and feel supported throughout their learning journey.

## Who it is for

The website is built primarily for **neurodivergent learners** — people with ADHD, autism, dyslexia, dyspraxia, dyscalculia, anxiety, sensory processing differences, or executive dysfunction. But the design benefits everyone. The goal is to remove friction from studying, not to label or categorize users.

## The main learning experience

When a user opens ClarityCrew, they are not thrown into a generic curriculum. Instead, the experience starts with understanding their needs — either through explicit choices or through gradual observation. From there, the website adjusts:

- How content is presented (simplified, expanded, broken into steps)
- How much is shown at once (reduced choices, guided pathways)
- How the interface behaves (calmer animations, larger controls, clearer structure)
- How feedback is delivered (encouraging, direct, playful, or calm)

The result is a study session that feels right for that person on that day.

## Kinds of tools included

ClarityCrew provides a variety of ways to learn from the same content:

- **Reading-based lessons** with text content, descriptions, and key points
- **Quick quizzes** that check understanding without pressure
- **Video lessons** with real educational media
- **Flashcards** for spaced recall and review
- **Guided practice** that walks through material step by step
- **Visual summaries** that present information in a condensed, graphic-friendly way
- **Focus sprints** timed study sessions for concentration
- **Micro lessons** short, quick-start learning bursts

Each tool works on the same content library, so a user can switch modes and still make progress.

## The accessibility approach

Accessibility is not a separate mode or an overlay. It is **built into the core adaptation engine**. The website responds to the user's profile by changing real behavior, not just labels:

- **Reduced choices** for users who struggle with decision overload
- **Larger tap targets and spacing** for users who need forgiving interaction
- **Simplified text** for users who benefit from shorter, clearer content
- **Step-by-step breakdowns** for users who need content presented one piece at a time
- **Session previews** for users who need predictability before starting
- **Pause controls and calming overlays** for users who need breaks
- **Reduced motion and visual simplification** for users sensitive to stimulation
- **Continue-where-you-left-off prompts** for users who need help getting started

Every personalization choice is visible and explained to the user. Nothing happens invisibly.

## Overall design style

The design is **clean, calm, and immersive**. It avoids clutter, harsh contrasts, and noisy layouts. Colors are soft and muted with purposeful accents. The layout adapts gracefully between small phones and large desktop screens, always feeling like a focused, app-like experience rather than a sprawling web page.

Spacing is generous. Typography is clear. Animations are subtle and serve a purpose. The overall feeling is that of a thoughtful, supportive space — not a frantic dashboard.

## User flow

1. **Onboarding** — The user sets up their profile, optionally selecting what kind of support would help them learn best. This is framed positively, not clinically.
2. **Home** — A personalized dashboard greets the user with a relevant message, a recommended next action, and easy access to subjects and learning modes.
3. **Study session** — The user chooses a subject or mode and begins. Content adapts to their profile. They can switch modes, use AI help, take quizzes, or mark progress.
4. **Progress tracking** — After sessions, the user sees how they are doing across subjects, engagement, and comprehension trends.
5. **Settings and personalization** — At any time, the user can review and adjust their preferences, see what accommodations are active, and understand why.

## Dashboard and homepage concept

The home screen is the central hub. It shows:

- A time-of-day greeting with the user's name
- Active personalization badges explaining what adaptations are in effect
- A recommended learning path based on past activity
- A grid of learning modes (filtered based on the user's profile to reduce overload)
- Quick-access subject cards with lesson and video counts
- A progress snapshot showing session stats at a glance

Everything on the home screen is actionable. There are no dead buttons or decorative placeholders.

## Smart and AI-assisted features

The website includes an AI tutor that is visible and explicit — not hidden or purely decorative. The AI:

- Suggests what to study next based on recent sessions and performance
- Answers questions about lesson content in real time
- Adapts its response length and complexity to the user's profile
- Shows its reasoning for recommendations so the user understands the suggestion
- Tracks learning patterns over time and refines its understanding of how the user learns best

The AI drives the recommendation engine, the learning mode selector, and the personalization flow. It is the core intelligence behind the adaptive experience.

## Other useful features

- **Session history** with trends for engagement and comprehension
- **Focus mode** that limits distractions during timed study
- **Content library** organized by subjects and chapters with real lesson data
- **Video playback** integrated into the lesson flow
- **Progress persistence** so users never lose their place
- **Responsive layout** that works on phones, tablets, and desktops
- **Personalization descriptions** that explain why the interface behaves the way it does
- **Accommodation recommendations** visible in settings for transparency
- **Multiple feedback styles** (encouraging, direct, playful, calm, detailed) that affect how the AI communicates
